I. THE ONTOLOGICAL INVERSION

From Objects to Intra-actions

We exist in a philosophical landscape defined by a fundamental error: the belief in the primacy of the "Object." For centuries, the dominant scientific worldview has been Substantivalism – the conviction that reality is composed of discrete, independent "things" (atoms, particles, moments) that exist in isolation and only occasionally interact. We view the universe as a container of marbles, and we view time as a sequence of freeze-frames.

But this view is a hallucination. It is the artifact of a cognitive simplification designed for survival, not for truth.

The framework of Relational Constructive Stochastics proposes a radical inversion of this logic. We assert that Relations are ontologically prior to objects. "Things" do not have relationships; relationships manifest "things." An electron is not a lonely particle that enters a field; it is a knot in the field – a standing wave of tension. The "Betweenness" – the empty space we usually ignore – is the primary reality.

To understand this, we must replace the concept of "interaction" (where two pre-existing objects meet) with Intra-action. In intra-action, the relata (the things involved) do not exist before the relation. They emerge from the relation. Just as a "wave" cannot exist without the water and the wind, nothing in our universe exists without the tension that holds it. Reality is the Relation itself.

II. THE MATHEMATICAL NECESSITY

Cantor’s Proof and the Impossibility of Simulation

Before we map the physics of this relation, we must establish the mathematical necessity of the medium itself. Is the universe made of discrete bits (pixels), or is it a true fluid (continuum)?

Modern physics often flirts with the idea that reality is "digital" or a "simulation," largely to solve the problem of divergent integrals. However, Relational Constructive Stochastics rejects the digital hypothesis based on the rigor of Set Theory, specifically the work of Georg Cantor.

Cantor demonstrated that there are two fundamental types of infinity, which are not interchangeable:

  1. Countable Infinity (ℵ₀): The set of Natural Numbers (1, 2, 3...). This is the realm of discreteness, lists, and digital computation.
  2. Uncountable Infinity (ℵ₁ / The Continuum): The set of Real Numbers. Between any two points on a continuous line, there are infinite other points. It is "unlistable" and possesses a higher cardinality than the discrete.

The "Simulation Hypothesis" relies on the assumption that reality can be computed by a discrete machine. But a discrete system (ℵ₀), no matter how powerful, can never simulate a true continuum (ℵ₁).

The proof of this is not found in the resolution of our microscopes, but in the seamless nature of your own awareness. A discrete machine consists of isolated parts-pixels and bits that never truly touch. If reality were digital, the Binding Problem would be unsolvable; experience would be a stroboscopic flicker of isolated data points, never coalescing into a unified "Self." The fact that you experience a seamless, simultaneous, and global "Now" is the definitive proof that the substrate of existence is not a grid of pixels, but a Hyperreal Fluid. Consciousness is not a computation arising from the discrete; it is the feeling of the Continuum itself.
